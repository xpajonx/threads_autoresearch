# Phase 2: Socratic Inquiry & Mental Models
Topic: Anatomi Ketakutan Terhadap Kesendirian

## 1. Mental Models
### Social Model
- **Focus**: Status and Belonging.
- **Inquiry**: Is the fear of solitude actually a fear of low social status? In ancestral environments, being alone signaled exclusion/expulsion from the tribe, which is a low-status marker.
- **Blind Spot**: Does the "fear of being seen alone" differ from the "fear of being alone"?

### Economic Model
- **Focus**: Resource Optimization.
- **Inquiry**: Group living as a "shared economy" for energy expenditure. Solitude is "expensive" because one must perform all survival tasks (vigilance, foraging, defense) alone.
- **Blind Spot**: The energy cost of hypervigilance in isolated individuals.

### Culture Model
- **Focus**: Historical Lineage.
- **Inquiry**: The transition from the "Hermit/Monk" (Solitude as Power) to the "Social Media User" (Solitude as Failure).
- **Blind Spot**: Philosophical shifts in the value of the "inner life."

### Digital Model
- **Focus**: Network Effects.
- **Inquiry**: How "Always-On" connectivity has rewired the brain's Default Mode Network to require constant external validation.
- **Blind Spot**: The "Phantom Limb" syndrome of digital disconnection.
